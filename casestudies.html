Project 03 · TRUXCARGO · Delhi, India · 2024

Building a WhatsApp chatbot that stopped the phone from ringing.

How I designed and deployed a WhatsApp automation system at TRUXCARGO — moving across three platforms to land on the right stack — and built a chatbot that handled FAQs, order tracking, and agent routing without human intervention.

80%+
Response time drop

The problem

Customers were calling to ask questions the business had already answered — just not where customers were looking.

At TRUXCARGO, our WhatsApp channel was the primary inbound touchpoint. The same questions came in constantly — order tracking, pickup availability, pricing, service areas. Every response was manual. The team was spending hours each day on messages that followed the exact same patterns.

Worse, queries were spilling over to the customer service phone line — calls that didn't need to happen if the right information was surfaced at the right moment.

The platform journey

Three platforms before finding the right fit.

1
Interakt — Started here. Good for basic broadcast messaging and simple auto-replies, but hit its ceiling quickly when we needed branching logic and conditional flows.
2
WATI — Moved here for more automation depth. The flows were more capable but reliability wasn't there — bots would break mid-conversation and leave users with no response.
3
DoubleTick — The right call. Advanced chatbot builder, stable automation flows, and proper agent re-routing. This is where we built what we were trying to build from the start.
What I built

A structured chatbot flow covering FAQs, order tracking, and intelligent agent handoff.

FAQ automation
Mapped the most common inbound queries into predefined flows with instant responses — no agent needed.
Order tracking flow
Built a dedicated order tracking path — customers got status updates directly through the bot, eliminating the most common reason people called customer service.
Smart agent routing
When a query fell outside scope, the flow routed to the right specific agent — sales to sales, operations to ops.
Ongoing optimisation
As bots occasionally broke down, I was close enough to the system to diagnose and fix quickly — keeping downtime minimal.
Impact

First response time dropped by over 80%. The phone stopped ringing as much.

Before the bot, average first response time on WhatsApp was measured in hours — dependent on team availability. After implementation, the bot responded in seconds. That improvement translated directly into a measurable reduction in inbound customer service calls.

What this built in me

Learning a new technology end-to-end — and staying ahead when it breaks.

1
Platform-switching isn't failure — it's iteration. Moving from Interakt to WATI to DoubleTick wasn't wasted effort. Each taught me what the right solution needed to do.
2
Automation systems need an owner, not just a builder. The bots broke. What mattered was that I understood the system well enough to fix it fast.
3
The best marketing automation removes friction customers didn't even tell you about. Nobody asked for WhatsApp order tracking — but once it worked, the call volume told the story.

Project 04 · Personal Build · @modifiedwhipss · 2025

I automated 9 years of manual Instagram posting — as a non-developer, in one week.

How I built a full Instagram automation pipeline from scratch — using Python, a Telegram bot, Claude AI, and a cloud server — to run a 9-year-old car modification account without touching my phone.

The background

Nine years of finding reels, writing captions, and posting manually. On a quiet Tuesday, I decided to change that.

I've been running @modifiedwhipss on Instagram since 2017. Finding modified Indian car reels, downloading them, writing captions, crediting creators, and posting — all manually, every time.

I'm a Digital Marketing professional. I use AI tools every day at work — Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini. But I'd never actually built anything myself. So I spent a week trying to change that: 6 hours a day, every day.

The build (and the problems)

It did not take one day.

1
Instagram blocks every cloud server. 401 errors everywhere. Sessions, cookies, proxies — all blocked. Two days gone just trying to download a reel.
2
Video format issues. Instagram delivers video and audio as separate streams. Merging them required a tool that refused to install on the server.
3
The AI captions were terrible until I rewrote the prompts. Generic, robotic, wrong tone. The prompt engineering mattered more than the model.
4
The bot crashed when two instances ran simultaneously. Fixed with a simple lock file — but it took hours to diagnose.
How it works now

Paste a link. Get a caption. Approve. Post.

Paste a link in Telegram
Drop any Instagram reel link into the Telegram bot — it downloads the video automatically.
Claude AI writes the caption
A custom-prompted Claude model writes the caption — crediting the original creator, adding the follow line, matching the account tone.
I approve with one tap
Review the caption and approve. Edit if needed, or just type /postnow.
It posts to Instagram
The bot posts the reel with caption, tags, and everything — in seconds. No phone. No app.
The real learning

I spent months telling brands to automate. Now I understand the gap between strategy and execution.

Production breaks differently than planning. Error logs at 2am teach you more than any course. The bottleneck is never what you expect. And the fact that I — a non-developer — built a working pipeline with a Python backend, cloud deployment, an AI model, and a Telegram approval interface means the barrier to building is lower than most marketers think.
